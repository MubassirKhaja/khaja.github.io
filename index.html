    MemeChat: Protecting User Privacy

    In my security research for MemeChat, a popular platform for meme sharing and creation, I uncovered a significant vulnerability in its API endpoints. The flaw allowed unauthorized access to sensitive user data, potentially exposing users' personal information and compromising their privacy. Upon identifying this breach, I took immediate steps to report it to MemeChat's development team.
    My contribution was recognized by the MemeChat team, earning me a Letter of Appreciation for identifying this critical flaw and helping improve the platform's overall security.

  • Security

    Apr 27, 2020

    TataSky: Bypassing Premium Channel Access

    While analyzing the TataSky DTH (Direct-To-Home) satellite TV platform, I uncovered a vulnerability that allowed unauthorized access to premium and paid channels without an active subscription. By hijacking session tokens, I demonstrated how attackers could exploit the vulnerability to view premium content, including Hotstar channels, for free.

    Key Highlights:
    Detected a session token hijacking vulnerability that allowed unauthorized access to paid and premium channels.

    Demonstrated how the vulnerability could bypass subscription checks, enabling access to restricted content.

    Coordinated with TataSky's security team to resolve the issue, preventing potential revenue loss and safeguarding the platform from future exploitation.

    This vulnerability could have caused significant financial damage and user dissatisfaction, but through timely identification and collaboration with the TataSky team, I helped ensure the platform's integrity.
